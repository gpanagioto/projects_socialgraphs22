'''American Dream''' ('''Shannon Carter''') is a <!--Do not add "fictional" as it is tautological; superheroes (and characters in general) are by definition implied to be fictionalized to some extent.-->[[superhero]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character was created by writer [[Tom DeFalco]] and artist [[Brent Anderson (comics)|Brent Anderson]]. Her first appearance was in the first volume of [[Marvel Comics]]' [[Marvel Comics 2|MC2]] series ''[[A-Next|A-Nex]]'' (August 1998).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lealos |first=Shawn S. |date=2021-07-30 |title=10 Most Important Marvel Multiverse Worlds From The Comics |url=https://screenrant.com/ten-most-important-marvel-multiverse-worlds-comics/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=ScreenRant |language=en-US}}</ref> Initially debuting as a civilian in ''A-Next'' #1, the character has been referred as "Shannon" in ''A-Next'' #3, before appearing in costume as a prospective team member during ''A-Next'' #4. Her costume is very similar to that of the comic book superhero [[Captain America]], and her initial weapons are throwing disks like [[Ricochet (comics)|Ricochet]]'s but later obtains the [[Captain America's shield|trademark shield]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jung |first=Michael |date=2020-06-30 |title=Future Captain America Has The LAST Job Fans Expect |url=https://screenrant.com/future-captain-america-shannon-carter/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=ScreenRant |language=en-US}}</ref> American Dream has appeared in two [[Limited series (comics)|mini-series]]. Her own comic book series, titled ''American Dream'' (May [[2008 in comics|2008]]), and ''Captain America Corps'' (June 2011).

==Fictional character biography==
[[File:A-Next no.4 (Cover art).jpg|thumb|left|upright|Shannon Carter as American Dream on the cover of ''[[A-Next]]'' #4 (1999). Artwork by Ron Frenz & Brett Breeding.]]
The niece of [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agent [[Sharon Carter]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.marveldatabase.com/Shannon_Carter_%28Earth-982%29 |title=Marvel Database entry for Shannon Carter |publisher=En.marveldatabase.com |date=2010-10-04 |access-date=2011-01-12}}</ref> Shannon Carter grew up idolizing Captain America.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The 20 Most Patriotic Comic Book Characters |url=https://www.gamespot.com/gallery/the-20-most-patriotic-comic-book-characters/2900-745/#15 |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=GameSpot |language=en-US}}</ref> She was hired by [[Edwin Jarvis]] as a tour guide for the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] Headquarters, at that time functioning only as a museum.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Morrison |first=Matt |date=2018-04-07 |title=Captain America: 15 Things Only True Marvel Fans Know About Nomad |url=https://screenrant.com/nomad-captain-america-trivia-infinity-war/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=ScreenRant |language=en-US}}</ref> When the new Avengers team was formed, she was determined to join.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Karbank |first=Octavio |date=2017-08-18 |title=8 Captain Americas Better Than Steve Rogers (And 7 Who Couldn't Handle It) |url=https://www.cbr.com/captain-americas-better-and-worse-than-steve-rogers/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=CBR |language=en-US}}</ref> She wears a costume based on Captain America's and carries on her arms disc weapons resembling miniature versions of his shield. Her strength and agility are honed by intensive physical training. After joining the team as the American Dream,<ref>''A-Next'' #4</ref> Shannon soon proved herself to be an effective Avenger, and when the team saved the original Avengers from a dark parallel world, Captain America himself judged her worthy to wield the shield of the alternate Captain.<ref>''A-Next'' #11</ref> She also proved to be an important character in defeating [[Galactus]] in the ''[[Last Planet Standing]]'' limited series. She, along with Stinger and Spider-Girl, took down Galactus from the inside. They survived the imminent explosion by shrinking to microscopic size.

Carter is the leader of the "Dream Team", consisting of herself, [[Bluestreak (comics)|Bluestreak]], [[Crimson Curse]] and [[Freebooter (comics)|Freebooter]]. All members of the Dream Team became members of the new Avengers team in ''A-Next'' #4. American Dream is also an ally of [[Spider-Girl (Mayday Parker)|Spider-Girl]], [[Earth Sentry]], [[Blacklight (MC2)|Blacklight]], [[Coal Tiger]], [[Argo the Almighty|Argo]] and Captain America.

American Dream has fought against the [[Sons of the Serpent]],<ref>''A-Next'' #4 and #9</ref> [[Seth (Marvel Comics)|Seth]], the [[Revengers]],<ref>''A-Next'' #12</ref> Fatal Force and [[Loki (Marvel Comics)|Loki]].<ref>''Last Hero Standing''</ref> When [[Superia]] launched a cross-temporal attack to eliminate Steve Rogers' post-war career as Captain America and take his place herself, American Dream was one of five heirs to Rogers' legacy that were assembled by the [[Contemplator (Marvel Comics)|Contemplator]] to thwart her plans, the others being Rogers early in his career, [[U.S. Agent (comics)|John Walker]] shortly after his own time as Cap, [[Bucky Barnes|Bucky]] during his time as Captain America, and Commander A from the twenty-fifth century.

==Powers and abilities==
Although a non-powered human, American Dream is in peak physical condition and is a skilled [[martial arts|martial artist]] and an Olympic level athlete.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mercado |first=Julia |date=2022-06-08 |title=7 Comic Characters We Could See Enter the MCU's Multiverse |url=https://collider.com/comic-characters-we-could-see-mcus-multiverse/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=Collider |language=en-US}}</ref> She has disc weapons similar to [[Ricochet (comics)|Ricochet]]'s and has a superb aim.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Friedman |first=Nicholas |date=2018-07-22 |title=Shield Of Dreams: The Very Best Captain Americas, Officially Ranked |url=https://www.cbr.com/captain-americas-ranked/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=CBR |language=en-US}}</ref> She now also carries a version of Captain America's shield, which she uses both defensively and offensively to great effect.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2020-06-30 |title=Future Captain America Has The LAST Job Fans Expect |language=en-US |work=ScreenRant |url=https://screenrant.com/future-captain-america-shannon-carter/ |access-date=2022-09-24}}</ref> Her fighting skills are matched by her leadership ability.<ref>{{Cite web |last=II |first=Walter Lovett |date=2022-09-16 |title=10 Most Muscular Women In Marvel Comics |url=https://www.cbr.com/most-muscular-women-marvel-comics/ |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=CBR |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=published |first=Jason Wiese |date=2021-03-06 |title=5 Marvel Characters Katherine Langford Would Be Perfect To Play |url=https://www.cinemablend.com/news/2563724/marvel-characters-katherine-langford-would-be-perfect-to-play |access-date=2022-09-24 |website=CINEMABLEND |language=en}}</ref>
