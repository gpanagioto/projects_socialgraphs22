'''Michael "Mickey" Fondozzi''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character has been depicted as an ally of the [[antihero]] the [[Punisher]].<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.empireonline.com/movies/features/punisher-complete-history/|title = The Punisher: a complete history|last = Williams|first = Owen|publisher = [[Empire (film magazine)|Empire]]|date = 28 January 2016|website = empireonline.com|access-date = 20 May 2016}}</ref> He was created by [[Chuck Dixon]] and [[John Romita Jr.]], and first appeared in ''[[The Punisher War Zone]]'' #1 (March 1992).

== Publication history ==
The character first appeared in a story-arc that spanned ''[[The Punisher War Zone]]'' #1-6, and was also present in #9-10, #23, #31, #41, and both of ''The Punisher War Zone'' Annuals. Fondozzi's appearances in other titles include ''[[The Punisher War Journal]]'' #45-47, #60-61, #64, #68, and #78-79, ''[[The Punisher (1987 series)|The Punisher]]'' vol. 2, #86, #97-100, and #102-104, ''The Punisher Summer Special'' #4, and ''[[Marvel Knights#Marvel Knights (team)|Marvel Knights]]'' #5 and #10-11.

== Fictional character biography ==
A career criminal living in [[Sheepshead Bay, Brooklyn|Sheepshead Bay]],<ref name = "PWJ77">{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = Mel Ruby|inker = Steve Moncuse, Mike Kraiger, and M. Peter Keating|colorist = John Kalisz|letterer = Vickie Williams|editor = Chris Cooper|story = Bound by Blood|title = The Punisher War Journal|volume = |issue = #77|date = 1 April 1995|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> Mickey claims to be [[Italians|Italian]], but is in fact [[Albanian people|Albanian]].<ref name = "PWZ1">{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = Only the Dead Know Brooklyn|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #1|date = March 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

While robbing a Chinese restaurant that served as a front for the local [[triad (organized crime)|triad]], Mickey and his crew are attacked by the Punisher, who leaves the crook alive while butchering everyone else. Via psychological torture, the Punisher coerces Mickey into sponsoring him (under the alias "Johnny Tower") for membership in the Carbones, a [[Brooklyn]] crime family he belongs to.<ref name = "PWZ1"/><ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = Blood in the Water|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #2|date = April 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> When Salvatore Carbone, one of the family's top men, becomes suspicious of Mickey and "Johnny", the two frame him for disloyalty, and are given permission to kill him by Salvatore's brother and the Carbone family boss, Julius. The attempt on Salvatore's life goes awry, and ends with him falling through the ice of a frozen lake, where he is later found by the authorities and admitted to a hospital as a [[John Doe]].<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = The Frame|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #3|date = May 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

Mickey and the Punisher's efforts to sabotage the Carbone family are ultimately discovered by Julius who orders his men to kill them, though the two are saved by [[Shotgun (comics)|Shotgun]].<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = Closer to the Flames|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #4|date = June 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref><ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = Feeding Frenzy|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #5|date = July 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> The Punisher allows Mickey to live on the condition that he become an informant for him, after Mickey helps him and Shotgun massacre the Carbones and the European syndicate bosses who had traveled to America to form a partnership with them.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Romita, Jr.|inker = Klaus Janson|colorist = Gregory Wright|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = The Carrion Eaters|title = The Punisher War Zone|volume = |issue = #6|date = Aug. 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

Mickey begins smuggling cigarettes in DeKalb, but his operation is shut down by the Punisher, who gives Mickey something better to do with his time by having him work with [[Microchip (comics)|Microchip]] to spy on high-ranking criminals attending a summit in [[Las Vegas]].<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Hebert|inker = Fred Fredericks|colorist = Glynis Oliver|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = The Vegas Idea|title = The Punisher War Journal|volume = |issue = #45|date = 10 Aug. 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref> Mickey and Microchip's investigation leads to them being captured by [[Hydra (comics)|Hydra]], though the two are able to stave off being executed by the organization by telling [[Werner von Strucker]] that they are members of the [[Secret Empire (organization)|Secret Empire]].<ref name ="PWJ46"/> The duo escape, track the Punisher down to a [[Nevada]] airport, and give him, [[Nomad (comics)|Nomad]], and [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] a drive back to New York City.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Chuck Dixon|penciller = John Hebert|inker = Fred Fredericks|colorist = Glynis Oliver|letterer = Jim Novak|editor = Don Daley|story = Say Goodbye to Vegas|title = The Punisher War Journal|volume = |issue = #47|date = 10 Oct. 1992|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

== Other versions ==

===Crossoververse (Earth-7642)===
Mickey is "a lousy little snitch" who is repeatedly accosted for information by the Punisher in ''The Punisher/Painkiller Jane''. [[Painkiller Jane]] shoots Mickey to death after he tells her that he sold the Punisher out to a crime lord named Vinnie Veronica.<ref>{{Cite comic|writer = Garth Ennis|penciller = Joe Jusko and Dave Ross|inker = Josef Rubinstein|colorist = Avalon Studios|letterer = Comicraft's Richard Starkings|editor = Nanci Dakesian and Jimmy Palmiotti|story = Lovesick|title = The Punisher/Painkiller Jane|volume = |issue = #1|date = Jan. 2001|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}}</ref>

== In other media ==
Mickey appears in the 2004 ''[[The Punisher (2004 film)|Punisher]]'' film, where the character is renamed '''Mickey Duka''' and portrayed by [[Eddie Jemison]]. An affiliate of the Saint crime family, Mickey is psychologically tortured (in a shot for shot recreation of his introduction in the comics)<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=3305|title = Behind the Scenes with ''Punisher'' Writer Michael France|last = Richards|first = Dave|publisher = Comic Book Resources|date = 23 March 2004|website = comicbookresources.com|access-date = 3 September 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/comics/news/a392482/chuck-dixon-criticises-disney-marvel-over-use-of-jim-starlins-thanos.html|title = Chuck Dixon criticizes Marvel for not giving Jim Starlin credit for Thanos' movie appearance|publisher = [[Digital Spy]]|date = 9 July 2012|website = digitalspy.co.uk|access-date = 27 May 2015}}</ref> by the Punisher into helping him wipe out the Saints as revenge for wiping out his family. Mickey, who actually despises the Saints as much as Castle does, willingly becomes his mole afterwards and helps him cause the Saints to destroy each other before Castle personally kills Howard Saint.<ref>{{cite AV media|people = [[Jonathan Hensleigh]] (Director)|date = 16 April 2004|title = [[The Punisher (2004 film)|The Punisher]]|medium = Motion picture|location = United States|publisher = [[Lions Gate Entertainment]]}}</ref>
