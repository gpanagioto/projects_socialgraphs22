'''Nathaniel Richards''' is a [[Character (arts)|fictional]] [[Time travel|time-traveling]] [[Mad scientist|scientist]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He is the father of [[superhero]] [[Reed Richards]], a founding member, and the leader of the [[Fantastic Four]]. He is the [[namesake]] of his descendant, the futuristic villain known as [[Kang the Conqueror]]; unlike his descendant, the original Nathaniel is more of an adventurer who has a genuine love for his son.

==Publication history==
Nathaniel Richards first appeared in ''[[The Fantastic Four (comic book)|The Fantastic Four]]'' #272 (November [[1984 in comics|1984]]) and was created by writer-artist [[John Byrne (comics)|John Byrne]].<ref>{{cite book |last1=DeFalco |first1=Tom |last2=Sanderson |first2=Peter |last3=Brevoort |first3=Tom |last4=Teitelbaum |first4=Michael |last5=Wallace |first5=Daniel |last6=Darling |first6=Andrew |last7=Forbeck |first7=Matt |last8=Cowsill |first8=Alan |last9=Bray |first9=Adam |title=The Marvel Encyclopedia |date=2019 |publisher=DK Publishing |isbn=978-1-4654-7890-0 |page=296}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
Dr. Nathaniel Richards was a successful scientist who specialized in multiple fields. He was married to [[Evelyn Richards|Evelyn]] and together had a son named Reed who also possessed an intellectual mind like his father. When Evelyn died, Nathaniel continued to raise his son right by supporting his scientific endeavors.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #570</ref> Later, Nathaniel is approached by the [[S.H.I.E.L.D.#Titles|Brotherhood of the Shield]] and joins their group. Along with [[Howard Stark]], the two meet a super powered individual named Leonid and help him resolve a dispute between the time traveling [[Leonardo da Vinci]] and [[Isaac Newton]] who had split the organization in two. These events somehow resulted in Nathaniel gaining the ability to travel through time.<ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #1–6</ref><ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' vol. 2 #1–4</ref>

Nathaniel found himself in the far future along with several other alternate versions of himself. The villain [[Immortus]] had gathered them together and forced them to kill each other off.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #581</ref> Nathaniel managed to flee and in the process left his family for good, but not before setting up his son Reed for life.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #271–272</ref>

Nathaniel ended up in another dimension where the Earth had become a desolate wasteland when the moon was destroyed. He married a woman named Cassandra who bore two children for him, one of which grew up to be [[Huntara]]. Cassandra turned out to be a power-mad individual using his technology to rule the remaining humans. The [[Fantastic Four]] and [[Wyatt Wingfoot]] arrived in this dimension where they defeated Cassandra and freed Nathaniel. After father and son had a heartfelt reunion, Nathaniel stayed behind to help rebuild.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #272–273</ref>

However, Nathaniel arrived in the present to kidnap [[Franklin Richards (Fantastic Four)|Franklin Richards]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #376</ref> Nathaniel aged Franklin to a teenager in an effort to refrain Franklin from having a child with [[Rachel Summers]]. Their child would eventually grow up to become the villain [[Hyperstorm]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #414</ref> Nathaniel would help Franklin and the Fantastic Four battle [[Aron (comics)|Aron the Rogue Watcher]] by rescuing [[Uatu]] the [[Watcher (comics)|Watcher]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #400</ref>

Nathaniel later trained [[Invisible Woman|Susan Storm]] to better master her powers.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #408</ref> 

Nathaniel returned to help Reed jumpstart the [[Future Foundation]], using the technology he gathered over the years.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #588</ref> He then helped them battle the [[Kree]] Army and the [[Celestial (comics)|Mad Celestials]].<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #600–604</ref><ref>''FF'' #12–15</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
While Nathaniel Richards has no superhuman powers, he does have scientific genius intellect and is also a skilled inventor in advanced machinery and devices.

Nathaniel Richards invented a device called the Time Platform and has grand experience as a time traveler. He also created his own armor which gives him super strength and durability. Nathaniel Richards also carries teleportation devices and energy weapons for defense.

==Other versions==
===What If?===
* In "What if the Fantastic Four were [[Cosmonaut]]s?", Nathaniel named his son Rudion and was interned in a [[Siberia]]n [[labor camp|camp]] after America lost faith in the [[USSR]].<ref>''What If: Fantastic Four'' #1</ref>
* In another reality based on [[Camelot]], Nathaniel Richards is a variation of [[Merlin (Marvel Comics)|Merlin]] to a version of [[Black Knight (comics)|Black Knight]].<ref>''What If?'' vol. 2 #97</ref>

===The Beast===
A version of Nathaniel Richards exist that was pulled into the far future with the other Nathaniels by [[Immortus]]. This version of Nathaniel, called the Beast, was a homicidal maniac who took the opportunity to slaughter all of his alternate counterparts. The Nathaniel of Earth-616 teams up with young college aged Reed Richards, Ben Grimm and Victor von Doom in order to defeat him.<ref>''Fantastic Four'' #581-582</ref>

===Ultimate Marvel===
In the [[Ultimate Marvel]] Universe, Reed's father is renamed '''Gary Richards''' and he does not have a scientific background. He also seems to despise his son's scientific mind, preferring his friend Ben Grimm's athletic abilities. When the [[Baxter Building]] think tank accepted Reed, Gary was glad that he was gone.<ref>''Ultimate Fantastic Four'' #1</ref> When [[Diablo (Marvel Comics)|Diablo]] kidnapped Reed's younger sister Enid, Gary went to Reed for help though he still resented him.<ref>''Ultimate Fantastic Four'' #42</ref> When Reed quit the Fantastic Four, Gary was there to greet him, not acting surprised.<ref>''Ultimatum Fantastic Four: Requiem''</ref> Gary, along with his wife Mary and two daughters, were killed in an explosion directed at the Richards household, later revealed to have been perpetrated by Reed himself, now the [[supervillain]]ous [[Maker (Reed Richards)|Maker]].<ref>''Ultimate Enemy'' #1</ref>
