'''Claire Temple''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She is primarily affiliated with the [[superhero]] [[Luke Cage]] and is one of his early love interests.

Starting in 2015, [[Claire Temple (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|a version of the character]], portrayed by [[Rosario Dawson]], has appeared in multiple episodes of the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] [[streaming television]] series, such as the first two seasons of ''[[Daredevil (TV series)|Daredevil]]'', an episode of the first season of ''[[Jessica Jones (TV series)|Jessica Jones]]'', ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'', the first season of ''[[Iron Fist (TV series)|Iron Fist]]'' and ''[[The Defenders (miniseries)|The Defenders]]''. She is a composite of Claire and the comic character [[Night Nurse (comics)|Night Nurse]].

==Publication history==
Claire Temple first appeared in ''[[Luke Cage, Hero for Hire]]'' #2 and was created by [[Archie Goodwin (comics)|Archie Goodwin]] and [[George Tuska]].

The character of African-American superhero Luke Cage was created shortly after [[Blaxploitation]] films emerged as a popular new genre.<ref name="back45">{{cite journal |last=Callahan |first=Timothy |date=December 2010 |title=Power Man and Iron Fist |journal=[[Back Issue!]] |issue=45 |pages=3–11 |publisher=[[TwoMorrows Publishing]]}}</ref>

==Fictional character biography==
===''Luke Cage''===
Luke Cage meets Dr. Claire Temple, who works with Dr. Noah Burstein at his 42nd Street storefront clinic.<ref name="LC2">{{Cite comic | writer=[[Archie Goodwin (comics)|Goodwin, Archie]] | penciller=[[George Tuska|Tuska, George]] | inker=[[Billy Graham (comics)|Graham, Billy]] | letterer=[[John Costanza|Costanza, John]] | editor=[[Stan Lee|Lee, Stan]] | story=Vengeance is Mine! | title=[[Luke Cage, Hero for Hire]] | volume=1 | issue=2 | date=August 1972 | publisher=[[Marvel Comics]] | location=[[New York, NY]] }}</ref> Cage has been shot by hitmen sent by the criminal [[Diamondback (Willis Stryker)|Diamondback]], and Claire is surprised to find him only bruised by the bullets.<ref name="LC2"/> She is subsequently kidnapped by Diamondback, who Cage discovers is his old friend Willis Stryker, the man who had framed Cage for the crime that sent him to prison.<ref name="LC2"/> Cage frees Claire but Diamondback is killed by one of his own weapons.<ref name="LC2"/> Claire corroborates Cage's story with the police,<ref>{{cite comic|date=October 1972|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Mark of the Mace!|volume=1|issue=3}}</ref> and the two become romantically involved.<ref>{{cite comic|date=December 1972|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Cry Fear...Cry Phantom!|volume=1|issue=4}}</ref> In 1973, Claire is surprised to see two grateful clients kissing Cage,<ref>{{cite comic|date=February 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Knights and White Satin!|volume=1|issue=6}}</ref> but spending Christmas Eve together they are assaulted twice by the criminal Marley, whom Cage later subdues.<ref>{{cite comic|date=March 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Jingle Bombs!|volume=1|issue=7}}</ref> As Claire and Cage continue dating,<ref>{{cite comic|date=April 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Crescendo!|volume=1|issue=8}}</ref> Dr. Burstein—who is partially responsible for giving Cage his powers—continues to keep the secret from Claire (''[[Daily Bugle]]'' reporter Phil Fox) that Cage escaped from prison.<ref>{{cite comic|date=July 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Where There's Life...!|volume=1|issue=11}}</ref> Cage is tortured by his complicity in the death of the criminal Lionfang, but Claire helps him to realize that the villain brought it upon himself.<ref>{{cite comic|date=September 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=The Claws of Lionfang|volume=1|issue=13}}</ref> Having discovered Cage's secret but unable to publish it, Fox teams up with Cage's prison guard nemesis Billy Bob Rackham to get revenge on Cage by kidnapping Claire.<ref name="LC14">{{cite comic|date=October 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Retribution!|volume=1|issue=14}}</ref> They kidnap the wrong woman, Fox is killed and the police find Claire holding the murder weapon.<ref name="LC14"/> Cage creates a distraction to talk to Claire in custody, and she tells him that she now knows he is an escaped convict.<ref name="LC15">{{cite comic|date=November 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Retribution Part II|volume=1|issue=15}}</ref> Cage vows to find Fox's real killer.<ref name="LC15"/> Rackham is revealed as the murderer, exonerating Claire, who is reunited with Cage.<ref>{{cite comic|date=December 1973|title=Luke Cage, Hero for Hire|story=Shake Hands with Stiletto!|volume=1|issue=16}}</ref>

In the now-retitled ''[[Luke Cage, Power Man]]'' #18 (April 1974), Cage mourns his ex-girlfriend Reva Connors, who had been killed because of his rivalry with Willis Stryker.<ref name="LC18">{{cite comic|date=April 1974|title=[[Luke Cage, Power Man]]|story=Havok on the High Iron!|volume=1|issue=18}}</ref> Despite Cage's belief that everyone who gets close to him dies, he and Claire decide to stay together.<ref name="LC18"/> The relationship continues for years, with Claire often in danger because of her connection to Cage, and by 1976 his chosen profession has caused much conflict between them.<ref>{{cite comic|date=December 1976|story=Big Brother Wants You...Dead!|title=Power Man|volume=1|issue=38}}</ref> Cage is finally exonerated for the crime that originally put him in prison, but in the retitled ''[[Power Man and Iron Fist]]'' #50 (April 1978), Claire can no longer handle him constantly being in danger, and they separate.<ref>{{cite comic|date=April 1978|story=Freedom!|title=[[Power Man and Iron Fist]]|volume=1|issue=50}}</ref>

Claire and Cage meet again in 1982, first at a crime scene,<ref>{{cite comic|date=February 1982|story=Slasher!|title=Power Man and Iron Fist|volume=1|issue=78}}</ref> and then when she treats the injuries of his girlfriend, Harmony Young.<ref>{{cite comic|date=August 1982|story=Revenge!|title=Power Man and Iron Fist|volume=1|issue=84}}</ref> Cage inadvertently insults Claire while she is treating his friend, [[Rafael Scarfe]], in 1983.<ref>{{cite comic|date=August 1983|story=Some Kind of Hero!|title=Power Man and Iron Fist|volume=1|issue=96}}</ref> Soon after, he decides to quit fighting crime because the local citizens seem ungrateful and derisive, but she convinces him how important his work is.<ref>{{cite comic|date=September 1983|story=The Coming of the She Beast!|title=Power Man and Iron Fist|volume=1|issue=97}}</ref> Later in the series, Claire treats an injured [[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]].<ref>{{cite comic|date=December 1983|story=Soul Games!|title=Power Man and Iron Fist|volume=1|issue=100}}</ref>

===Other appearances===
In ''[[Bill Foster (comics)|Black Goliath]]'' #1 (February 1976), Bill Foster/Black Goliath recalls his marriage to his college sweetheart Claire and their eventual separation.<ref>{{Cite comic | writer=[[Tony Isabella|Isabella, Tony]] | penciller=[[George Tuska|Tuska, George]] | inker=[[Vince Colletta|Colletta, Vince]] | colorist=Wolfman, Michele | letterer=Crespi, Dan and Irving Watanabe | coartists=[[Frank Giacoia|Giacoia, Frank]] (cover), [[Ron Wilson (comics)|Wilson, Ron]] (cover) | editor=[[Marv Wolfman|Wolfman, Marv]] | story= | title=[[Bill Foster (comics)|Black Goliath]] | volume=1 | issue=1 | date=February 1976 | publisher=Marvel Comics | location=New York, NY }}</ref>

Claire treats a fully costumed [[Spider-Man]] following his fight with [[Man-Thing]] in ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'' #123 (November 1982).<ref>{{cite comic|date=November 1982|story=Rivers of Blood|title=[[Marvel Team-Up]]|volume=1|issue=123}}</ref>

As part of the ''[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]'', Claire is a doctor who specializes in dealing with super-powered humans and bionic implants.<ref>{{cite comic |title=Captain America: Sam Wilson |issue=16 |date=December 2016}}</ref> She first appears in December 2015 in ''Captain America: Sam Wilson'' #4, where she treats [[Falcon (comics)|Captain America (Sam Wilson)]] when he is transformed into a werewolf by [[Karl Malus]]<ref>{{cite comic |title=Captain America: Sam Wilson |issue=4 |date=December 2015 }}</ref> and is later shown to be in regular contact with [[Misty Knight]].{{issue|date=January 2017}}

After Rage had been arrested upon being accused of a pawn shop robbery done by [[Speed Demon (comics)|Speed Demon]] and [[Man Mountain Marko]] and is then beaten up in the penitentiary's Z Block by the super-powered prisoners which he previously apprehended, he was taken to the hospital where Sam is told by Claire Temple that Rage will not survive due to the extensive brain damage he sustained.<ref>''Captain America: Sam Wilson'' #2</ref>

During the ''[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]'' storyline, Claire Temple was in a Darkforce-covered Manhattan trying to tend to the ill civilians, as she tells the [[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]] that the hospital has limited medical supplies.<ref>''Secret Empire'' #2</ref>
