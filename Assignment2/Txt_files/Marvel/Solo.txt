'''James Bourne''', also known as '''Solo'''  is a [[fictional character]], appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character first appeared in ''Web of Spider-Man'' #19 in October 1986 and was created by writer [[David Michelinie]] and artist [[Marc Silvestri]].

A former citizen of the United States who renounced his citizenship, Solo works as a [[bodyguard]] and [[counter-terrorism]] operative. He is a master hand-to-hand combatant and expert marksman, and utilises a range of conventional weapons, although he also possesses limited [[teleportation]] abilities, allowing him to "jump" from place to place for short distances. He is known for his [[catchphrase]], "While Solo lives, terror dies!"

==Publication history==
{{expand section|date=June 2016}}
Solo first appeared in ''Web of Spider-Man'' #19 (October 1986). He was created by writer [[David Michelinie]] and artist [[Marc Silvestri]].

Solo has had his own self-titled 4-issue limited series in 1994, which guest-starred Spider-Man.

After appearing in ''[[Mercs for Money|Deadpool and the Mercs for Money]]'', he was given a second 5-issue limited series in 2016.

==Fictional character biography==
Born in the United States but having renounced his citizenship to any country, Solo works as a [[counter-terrorism]] operative, and makes limited appearances in the Marvel Comics universe. He is known for his [[catchphrase]], "While Solo lives, terror dies!"

In his first appearance, Solo teleported inside a foreign embassy in West Germany and killed all the terrorists inside.<ref>''Web of Spider-Man'' #19. Marvel Comics.</ref> He next foiled [[ULTIMATUM (comics)|ULTIMATUM]]'s plot to destroy the [[Arc de Triomphe]] in Paris.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #323. Marvel Comics.</ref> He also shot ULTIMATUM terrorists who were trying to destroy Ellis Island, and then joined forces with [[Spider-Man]] to capture ULTIMATUM's commanding officer.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #324. Marvel Comics.</ref> Outside [[Barcelona]], Spain, Solo assassinated Toro Mendoza, leader of the Cascan separatists.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #24. Marvel Comics.</ref>

When the [[Sinister Six]] reform and defeat both Spider-Man and the [[Hulk]], Solo aids the web-slinger in his second fight against the super villain team. Things take a turn for the worse when [[Mysterio]] uses an illusion to make Solo believe he is attacking the Six, when in reality he is attacking Spider-Man while the Six escape.<ref>''Spider-Man'' #20-21. Marvel Comics.</ref> Solo escapes and Spider-Man is rescued by [[Crimson Commando|Cyborg X]] and [[Deathlok]].<ref>''Spider-Man'' #21. Marvel Comics.</ref> Soon after, Solo rejoins Spider-Man to aid him in a final assault on the Six aided by the Hulk, [[Ghost Rider (Danny Ketch)|Ghost Rider]], [[Sleepwalker (comics)|Sleepwalker]], [[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]], Deathlok and the [[Fantastic Four]]. With the villains defeated, Solo disappears.<ref>''Spider-Man'' #23. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Solo later fought [[Tarantula (Marvel Comics)#Luis Alvarez|La Tarantula]].<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #88. Marvel Comics.</ref> He defeated a Sicilian crime syndicate,<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #26. Marvel Comics.</ref> and then joined forces with Spider-Man against the agents of the [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]] and the [[Red Skull]].<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #367. Marvel Comics.</ref> A rematch with La Tarantula ended with each combatant wrongly believing he had killed the other.<ref>''Marvel Comics Presents'' #124. Marvel Comics.</ref> Solo then joined forces with [[Black Cat (Marvel Comics)|Black Cat]] against the terrorist organization called ARES, and stopped their money laundering operation. He encountered their leader Deathstorm who revealed he had ties with Solo's past.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man Annual'' #27. Marvel Comics.</ref> Solo later joined forces with [[Nick Fury]] against the [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]].<ref>''S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #44-45. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Solo also once helped Spider-Man take on criminally-trained specialist versions of [[Captain America]], [[Hawkeye (Clint Barton)|Hawkeye]], and Spider-Man himself.<ref>''The Amazing Spider-Man'' #367. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Later, Solo assists dozens of other heroes in battling a seemingly rampaging [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]] (he was being mentally influenced). He confronts Wolverine, side-by-side with [[Cardiac (comics)|Cardiac]]. Solo is swiftly defeated, suffering deep lacerations in the process. Cardiac is subdued by falling masonry.<ref>''Wolverine'' vol. 2 #134 (February 1999). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Solo is hired by [[G. W. Bridge]] to join the new [[Six Pack (comics)|Six Pack]] alongside Hammer, [[Domino (comics)|Domino]], [[Anaconda (comics)|Anaconda]], and [[Constrictor (comics)|Constrictor]] in their mission to take down [[Cable (comics)|Cable]]. Like Bridge and Hammer, Solo is captured and placed in suspended animation. He is eventually released.<ref>''[[Cable & Deadpool]]'' #7-12. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the "[[Civil War (comic book)|Civil War]]" storyline, Solo sides with other heroes who oppose registration, including [[Battlestar (comics)|Battlestar]] and [[Typeface (comics)|Typeface]]. While waiting to make contact with the resistance led by Captain America, Solo and the others are arrested by [[Iron Man]], [[Ms. Marvel]], [[Wonder Man]] and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agents.<ref>''Civil War'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Solo has been identified as one of the 142 registered superheroes.<ref>''[[Avengers: The Initiative]]'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref><ref>[http://www.marvel.com/news/comicstories.947 ''Avengers: The Initiative'' #1 Character Map]</ref> Solo was hired along with Clay, as bodyguards to protect Wally and Molly, cheery twins who sing anti-Mutant hate-songs. They chained up [[M (Marvel Comics)|M]], tied up [[Siryn]] and taped her mouth shut, and locked both women in a desert bunker after they tried to round two kids up in order to enforce the kids grandparents' court granted visitation rights.<ref>''X-Factor'' vol. 2 #22-23. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the "[[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]]" storyline, Solo was hired by a man later revealed to be [[Bullseye (comics)|Bullseye]]'s [[Kingmaker (comics)|father]] to capture Bullseye. Bullseye threw a shard of glass into Solo's left eye, but Solo survived and managed to capture Bullseye after tasering him.<ref>''Dark Reign: Hawkeye'' #4. Marvel Comics.</ref> The assassins chasing [[Elektra (comics)|Elektra]] attempt to hire Solo to assist but fail.<ref>''Dark Reign: Elektra'' #4-5 (March 2009). Marvel Comics.</ref>

Following the eight month [[ellipsis (narrative device)|ellipsis]] after the "[[Secret Wars (2015 comic book)|Secret Wars]]" storyline, Solo is seen as part of [[Deadpool]]'s new [[Mercs for Money]]. He attempts to steal a classified drive in Seoul, and ends up in a fight with the Korean heroine White Fox.<ref>''Deadpool'' vol. 4 #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> Solo takes a specific interest in Deadpool's morality, such as when he urges him to destroy a robot whose knowledge of the future threatens innocent lives.<ref>''Deadpool & The Mercs for Money'' #5 (2016). Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the "[[Iron Man 2020 (event)|Iron Man 2020]]" event, Solo appears as a member of [[Force Works]]. During a raid on a robot hideout, one of the robots self-destructs when cornered by [[War Machine]] and [[Gauntlet (Joseph Green)|Gauntlet]]. [[Maria Hill]] mentioned to War Machine that Solo quit because he felt that War Machine was not teammate material.<ref>''2020 Force Works'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Solo has somewhat limited [[teleportation]] abilities, allowing him to "jump" from place to place for short distances. There appears to be a limit to the distance and the amount of time between Solo's teleports. He has a gifted intellect, and is a master of many forms of hand-to-hand combat. He is highly adept in the use of conventional weapons and firearms and is an expert marksman.

Solo wears bullet-proof quilted Kevlar with pouches to hold weapons and ammunition. He carries an arsenal of portable conventional weaponry, including sub-machine guns, automatic rifles, automatic pistols, hand grenades, combat knives, etc., and has been known to use ninja climbing claws.
