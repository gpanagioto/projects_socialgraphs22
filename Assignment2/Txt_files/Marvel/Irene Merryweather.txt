'''Irene Merryweather''', also known as the '''Chronicler''', is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. A former reporter, she now acts as a friend and confidante of [[Cable (comics)|Cable]] and was formerly his Chief of Staff at Providence before its destruction.

==Fictional character biography==
Irene always wanted to be a serious reporter working for the ''[[Daily Bugle]]'', but she got stuck working for the [[Tabloid (newspaper format)|tabloid]] ''The Inquiring Eye''. When asked by her editor to investigate [[Sebastian Shaw (comics)|Sebastian Shaw]] and the [[Hellfire Club (comics)|Hellfire Club]], Irene gets drawn into a conspiracy to kill [[Cable (comics)|Cable]].{{issue|date=March 2013}} Irene returns to her office, but finds her co-workers killed by Hellfire Club agents, who attempt to kill her. Cable appears and saves her.{{issue|date=March 2013}} He tells Irene that he is from the future and hires Irene to be his chronicler. Cable wants Irene to write down his life so that other people may know about him and his missions, should he get killed.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Irene accepts, but soon finds out that working with Cable is not easy; Cable leaves her behind in [[Switzerland]], when he feels that his mission endangers her and she has to return to [[New York City]] on her own.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Over the next few months, Irene and Cable become close friends, though she often disagrees with him and calls him on bad decisions.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Working for Cable, she also is a witness to many incredible events and lands a job as a reporter for the Daily Bugle by writing about them.{{issue|date=March 2013}} During this time, Irene investigates the history of the Hellfire Club and Sebastian Shaw offers her a place in the Hellfire Club in return for her silence. Irene refuses.<ref>''Cable'' #48-95</ref><ref>''[[X-Men]]:The Hellfire Club'' #1-4</ref>

When Cable renames himself ''Soldier X'' and disappears, Irene starts to worry about him. Her work suffers as a result, but one year later she receives a message from him. He sends her his diaries and confides in her.<ref>''Soldier X'' #1-7</ref>

When he returns as Cable, she interviews the would-be [[mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] [[Messiah]].{{issue|date=March 2013}} She thinks Cable's power is getting to his head and calls him on it, even bringing a gun to her office to protect herself from him, should he go insane.{{issue|date=March 2013}} When Cable secretly uses a virus to turn everybody on Earth pink, only to publicly restore them later to normal, he is lauded as a hero.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Irene is fired though for bringing a gun to work.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Without a job, Irene moves to Providence, an island-state created by Cable to be an utopia.{{issue|date=March 2013}} She takes over administrative duties in the city and basically runs the whole city when Cable is on his missions.{{issue|date=March 2013}} Irene even learns to put up with [[Deadpool (comics)|Deadpool]], Cable's new associate, who often expresses a romantic interest in her.{{issue|date=March 2013}} She doesn't return his feelings, seeing Deadpool as an idiot.<ref>''[[Cable & Deadpool]]'' #1</ref>

After the destruction of Providence by the Hecatomb, she is attacked by [[Sabretooth (comics)|Sabretooth]] within the ruins of the island but is rescued by the timely intervention of Deadpool, leaving the island before it detonates.{{issue|date=March 2013}} She later reappears in the final issue of Cable and Deadpool during a symbiote-dinosaur attack on New York.<ref>''[[Cable & Deadpool]]'' #50 (April 2008)</ref>

Cable's evil clone Stryfe later forces Deadpool to murder Irene when he threatens the lives of Wade's family.<ref>''Despicable Deadpool'' #292</ref>

==In other media==
*Irene Merryweather makes a cameo in ''[[Deadpool 2]]'' played by Sonia Sunger. She reports on [[Firefist (comics)|Russell Collins]]' attack on the Essex House for Mutant Rehabilitation and then when the [[X-Men]], consisting of [[Negasonic Teenage Warhead]], [[Colossus (comics)|Colossus]] and [[Deadpool (comics)|Deadpool]] arrive to stop him.
