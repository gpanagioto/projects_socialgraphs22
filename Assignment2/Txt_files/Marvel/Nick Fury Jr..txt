'''Nicholas Joseph''' "'''Nick'''" '''Fury Jr.''' ('''Marcus Johnson''') is a [[Fictional character|fictional]] [[comic book]] character appearing in books published by [[Marvel Comics]]. He is a son and successor of former [[United States Army|U.S. Army]] hero/super-spy and the intelligence agency [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] director [[Nick Fury]].<ref name="Scars5">''Battle Scars'' #5. Marvel Comics.</ref> The character first appeared in ''[[Battle Scars (comic book)|Battle Scars]]'' #1 (January 2012), which was written by [[Matt Fraction]], [[Christopher Yost|Chris Yost]], and [[Cullen Bunn]], and penciled by [[Scot Eaton]].

==Fictional character biography==
[[File:Marcus Johnson Nick Fury jr.png|thumb|upright|left|Nick Fury Jr. in ''Battle Scars'' #6 (April 2012). Art by [[Scot Eaton]].]]
Marcus Johnson was raised in Atlanta, Georgia, by his mother Nia Jones. At the age of 18, Marcus passed on numerous college football scholarships in order to enlist in the US Army, serving in [[Iraq War|Iraq]]. After his enlistment, he earned a degree in philosophy from the [[University of Georgia]]. Marcus then re-enlisted in the Army, achieving the rank of staff sergeant.<ref>''Battle Scars'' #2. Marvel Comics (February 2012).</ref>

While serving in [[War in Afghanistan (2001–present)|Afghanistan]] with [[2nd Ranger Battalion|2nd Battalion]], [[75th Ranger Regiment]], Marcus is told that his mother was murdered back in the United States. He returns home and is attacked by the men who killed his mother, and by [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]], but is saved by [[Captain America]]. [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agents arrive and take him to one of their facilities; after a while Johnson, comes to feel he is being held there against his will and escapes,<ref>''Battle Scars'' #2. Marvel Comics.</ref> but is caught by Taskmaster once again. Before he can obtain any information, a man in a mask saves him, but then flees. Johnson catches up to the man, who is revealed to be his father, [[Nick Fury]].<ref>''Battle Scars'' #4. Marvel Comics.</ref> Fury and Johnson are then captured by the organization [[Leviathan (Marvel Comics)|Leviathan]]'s former member Orion, who has Johnson's left eye cut out and confirms that Johnson has the Infinity Formula in his DNA, which has given Fury his long lifespan. Fury helps Johnson escape, but Fury himself has his blood transfused to Orion so Orion's youth can be restored, draining Fury's remaining Infinity Formula. Johnson saves Fury with the help of his Ranger friend [[Phil Coulson]], and later kills Orion. After convalescing, Johnson is given Steve Rogers's Super Soldier uniform to wear. As a new agent of S.H.I.E.L.D., he is also informed of his birth name, Nick Fury Jr.<ref name=scars6>''Battle Scars'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Fury and Coulson make a [[cameo appearance]] in ''[[Kaine Parker|Scarlet Spider]]'' #5,<ref>[[Scarlet Spider (comic book)#Volume 2|''Scarlet Spider'' vol. 2]] #5. Marvel Comics.</ref> and are part of the [[framing sequence]] in ''[[Marvel NOW!]] Point One''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=user_review&id=5287|title=Review: Marvel NOW! Point One|publisher=[[Comic Book Resources]]|date=17 October 2012}}</ref>

In a 2013 storyline as part of the [[Marvel NOW!]] branding, Nick, Coulson, and [[Maria Hill]] form the S.H.I.E.L.D. version of the [[Secret Avengers]] with [[Hawkeye (Clint Barton)|Hawkeye]] and [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)|Black Widow]]. Fury is involved in their first mission, which involved fighting a group of [[al-Qaeda]] terrorists.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> Fury then joins the Secret Avengers to raid Bagalia in order to recruit Taskmaster. While the Secret Avengers are fighting the [[Masters of Evil]], Fury manages to pay off [[Crossfire (comics)|Crossfire]] to free Taskmaster from his imprisonment.<ref>''Secret Avengers'' vol. 2 #2. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the 2014 "[[AXIS (comics)|AXIS]]" storyline, Nick was with S.H.I.E.L.D. when the organization has gathered Captain America to discuss what was to become of the [[Red Skull]], now that the Stark Sentinels had been dismantled and the concentration camps torn down. Fury tried to convince [[Falcon (comics)|Sam Wilson]] to hand him over. But under the inversion spell's influence, Wilson was violent and punched him before leaving.<ref>''Avengers & X-Men: AXIS'' #4. Marvel Comics.</ref>

During the 2016 "[[Civil War II]]" storyline, Fury is on a mission to neutralize a Hydra cell that is posing as a S.H.I.E.L.D. squadron. One of the visions of the [[Inhumans|Inhuman]] [[Ulysses Cain]], which were believed to predict the future, indicated that the Hydra cell would launch a deadly attack on S.H.I.E.L.D. When heading to a base in Arizona, Fury is attacked by actual agents instead of Hydra infiltrators as suspected. In order to find the traitors, Fury fakes his death goes into hiding.<ref>''Civil War II: Choosing Sides'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> His investigation takes him to the S.H.I.E.L.D. base Ulu in Alaska,<ref>''Civil War II: Choosing Sides'' #2. Marvel Comics.</ref> where he encounters the unknown mastermind behind the plot, who then escapes.<ref>''Civil War II: Choosing Sides'' #3. Marvel Comics.</ref> Upon infiltrating the base Ogma, Fury downloads classified data,<ref>''Civil War II: Choosing Sides'' #4. Marvel Comics.</ref> which leads him to the underground base Kratos, where he encountered a rogue [[Life Model Decoy]] of his father named Leader, who states that Cain's prediction does not involve the Hydra cell, and that it will cost him his life. Leader learns about Cain's abilities, and wants neutralize Fury in order to take over S.H.I.E.L.D. In order to stop Leader, Nick destroys a central support beam the collapses Kratos, killing Leader.<ref>''Civil War: Choosing Sides'' #5. Marvel Comics.</ref> Upon surviving the collapse, Nick salvages Leader's head so that he can hack it and learn how he discovered his plan. Nick forgives Hill for sending him on a mission that would've resulted in his death, and states that he is not ready to return to S.H.I.E.L.D. yet.<ref>''Civil War II: Choosing Sides'' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>

Upon his return to S.H.I.E.L.D. as a top-ranking agent, Fury infiltrates the French Riviera, where he ends up in a cat-and-mouse game with Hydra Agent Frankie Noble.<ref>''Nick Fury'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>

In the aftermath of the 2017 "[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]" story line, S.H.I.E.L.D. was disbanded but Fury continued to work as an independent operative.  Fury observes [[Punisher|Frank Castle]] slay every Hydra agent in an abandoned warehouse as a way to atone for siding with Hydra.<ref>''Secret Empire Omega'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> Fury then gives Castle access to the [[War Machine]] armor for a [[plausible deniability|deniable]] operation against a rogue Eastern European state caught using old S.H.I.E.L.D. resources.<ref>''Punisher'' #218. Marvel Comics.</ref>  Later he imprisons Ripley Ryan (aka Star) bearer of the Reality Gem. <ref>Black Cat Vol 2 #8</ref>

==Powers and abilities==
Nick Fury Jr. initially appears to have no superhuman qualities but he inherited his father's Infinity Formula at birth, slowing his aging process, speeding his healing time, and granting him peak human physical fitness.<ref name=scars6/>

Fury was trained in [[espionage]] under Maria Hill.<ref>{{cite comic|writer=MacKay, Jed|artist=Vitti, Alessandro|title=[[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]]|volume=3|issue=5|story=The Rubicon Trigger, Part Five|date=May 2021|publisher=Marvel Comics}}</ref>
