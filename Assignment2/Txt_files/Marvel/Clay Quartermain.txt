'''Clay Quartermain''' is a [[fictional character]], a [[espionage|secret agent]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].

==Publication history==
Created by [[writer]]-[[artist]] [[Jim Steranko]], he [[first appeared]] in ''[[Strange Tales]]'' #163 (Dec. 1967).

Clay Quartermain appears as an agent of the fictional espionage agency [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]], beginning in the feature "[[Nick Fury|Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.]]" in Marvel Comics' ''[[Strange Tales]]'' in 1967, and continuing into the subsequent series ''Nick Fury, Agent of S.H.I.E.L.D.'' in 1968. He became the S.H.I.E.L.D. liaison to General [[Thunderbolt Ross]]'s "[[Hulkbusters]]" military program, and a supporting character in ''[[The Incredible Hulk (comic book)|The Incredible Hulk]]'' vol. 2, beginning with issue #187 (May 1975).<ref>Quartermain appeared in ''The Incredible Hulk'' vol. 2, #187-188, 192-200, 206-207, 209-210, 212-216, 219, 224, 226-228, 230-231, 233, 237-240, 243, 315, 322-327, 329-332, 334, 336-346, spanning May 1975 to August 1988 cover-dates, plus ''The Incredible Hulk Annual'' #15 (Oct. 1986)</ref> A [[Life Model Decoy]] (LMD) replica of Quartermain was a featured character in the 1988 [[miniseries]] ''[[Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.]]''. Quartermain has since had guest appearances in issues of ''[[Alias (comics)|Alias]]'', ''[[Cable (comics)|Cable]]'', ''[[Defenders (comics)|The Defenders]]'', ''[[Marvel Team-Up]]'', ''[[The Pulse (comics)|The Pulse]]'', ''[[Silver Sable|Silver Sable and the Wild Pack]]'', and the miniseries ''[[Secret War (comics)|Secret War]]''; in the "[[Nick Fury]]" feature in the omnibus ''Marvel Holiday Special'' (Jan. 1994); and in the "Elite Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D." feature in the one-shot ''Captain America 2000'' (Nov. 2000). He also led the S.H.I.E.L.D. Paranormal Containment Unit in the 2005â€“2006 series ''[[Nick Fury's Howling Commandos]]''.

==Fictional character biography==
Clay Quartermain is a high-ranking agent of the fictional espionage agency [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]], a "blond-haired, fast-talking, grinning [[Burt Lancaster]]" sort<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.geocities.com/area51/nebula/8650/amazingheroes.html |title=''Amazing Heroes'' #26 (July 1, 1983): "Fury of the Past: A Nick Fury Hero History" by Lou Mougin |access-date=October 25, 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091028022225/http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Nebula/8650/amazingheroes.html |archive-date=October 28, 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> who first worked with that organization's storied executive director [[Nick Fury]] during S.H.I.E.L.D.'s first conflict with would-be world tyrant the [[Yellow Claw (comics)|Yellow Claw]], later revealed to have been a [[robot]] simulacrum of that [[Chinese people|Chinese]]-national [[Mandarin (comics)|Mandarin]]. Quartermain later became part of the [[U.S. military]]'s "Hulkbusters" operation, which attempted to capture and contain the [[Hulk]].

He became a fugitive and traveled with [[Rick Jones (comics)|Rick Jones]] and the Hulk. He met his brother, a farmer. He testified in Congress about a mass murder committed by the [[supervillain]] known as the [[Leader (comics)|Leader]] who had detonated a gamma bomb in a town to obliterate.<ref>''The Incredible Hulk'' #337-343 (Nov. 1987 - April 1988)</ref>

Once more at S.H.I.E.L.D., Quartermain, along with many other agents, was seemingly killed by a self-aware, renegade "Deltan" variety of the agency's [[Android (robot)|artificial-human]] "[[Life Model Decoy]]s", and replaced by one such LMD.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #2 (July 1988)</ref> The most advanced of the Deltite LMDs, it rebelled upon learning it was not human, and after several confrontations, perished.<ref>''Nick Fury vs. S.H.I.E.L.D.'' #6 (Nov. 1988)</ref>

[[Retconned]] as a former romantic interest of [[Jessica Jones]], Quartermain later became leader of [[Nick Fury's Howling Commandos|S.H.I.E.L.D.'s Paranormal Containment Unit]]. He was the agent sent to inform Jennifer Walters ([[She-Hulk]]) that she had been pressed into service as a S.H.I.E.L.D. agent.<ref>''She-Hulk'' vol. 2, #14 (Jan. 2007)</ref> He next led a new incarnation of S.H.I.E.L.D.'s "Hulkbusters" unit, here She-Hulk, Agent Crimson, and [[Agent Cheesecake]].<ref>''She-Hulk'' #15 (March 2007)</ref> Clay and his team then defeated the electrical entity [[Zzzax]].<ref>''She-Hulk'' vol. 2, #17 (April 2007)</ref>

Quartermain remained an ally of Jones, using her to bring down a conspiracy against the [[President of the United States]].<ref>''Alias'' #1-4 (Nov. 2001 - Feb. 2002)</ref> He assisted Jessica in the investigation of [[Spider-Woman (Mattie Franklin)|Mattie Franklin]], a young female superhero trapped in mysterious circumstances.<ref>''Alias'' #16 (Jan. 2003)</ref> Quartermain later led a S.H.I.E.L.D. unit that rescued Jones from a HYDRA recruitment attempt.<ref>''[[The Pulse (comics)|The Pulse]]'' #6-9 (2005)</ref> A flashback reveals Clay befriended Jessica while she was recovering from a months-long ordeal with the Purple Man.<ref>''Alias'' #26 (November 2003)</ref>

At a [[meatpacking]] plant, Quartermain was found, apparently dead in a suspected Red Hulk attack. The commanding officer of the crime scene was his old friend [[Gabe Jones]].<ref>''Hulk'' vol. 2, #4 (June 2008)</ref> It was later revealed, however, that his actual murderer was the now-insane [[Doc Samson]]. Quartermain had inadvertently stumbled upon a plot hatched by Red Hulk and Samson, and threatened to launch a full-scale investigation. This involved the [[Life Model Decoy]] of General [[Thunderbolt Ross]], seemingly killed during a fight with the Red Hulk; Ross was actually the Red Hulk himself.<ref>''Hulk'' vol. 2, #23 (June 2010)</ref>

==Other versions==
The [[Ultimate Marvel]] version of Clay Quartermain appears as a S.H.I.E.L.D. field agent.<ref>''Ultimate Spider-Man'' #27</ref> He was seen escorting [[Spider-Man (Ultimate Marvel character)|Peter Parker]] out of school to respond to a [[Ultimate Six|major threat]].<ref>''Ultimate Six'' #3 (2003)</ref>
