'''David "D.W." Griffith''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].  He is depicted as an ally of [[Luke Cage]].

The character was portrayed by Jeremiah Richard Craft in the television series ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'' set in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]].

==Publication history==
{{Expand section|date=February 2018}}
The character, created by [[Archie Goodwin (comics)|Archie Goodwin]] and [[George Tuska]], first appeared in ''Hero for Hire'' #2 (Aug. 1972).

==Fictional character biography==
He operated the Gem Theater and rented out a room for Luke Cage when he needed a place to stay. Despite the theater being a constant source of destruction, Griffith remained a faithful friend to Cage mostly because he considered him his only friend.<ref>''Power Man'' #21</ref> Griffith was also a film student and wanted to produce his own production company.<ref>''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #73</ref> When it seemed that he was going nowhere, Griffith attempted to commit suicide, but a mysterious stranger, implied to be [[Moon Knight]], convinced him not to do himself in and be happy.<ref>''Power Man and Iron Fist'' #88</ref> His theater was used a base for the [[Mighty Avengers]].<ref>''The Mighty Avengers'' vol. 2 #4</ref>

==In other media==
Dave Griffith makes a recurring appearance on ''[[Luke Cage (TV series)|Luke Cage]]'' played by Jeremiah Richard Craft. He is black, as opposed to Caucasian like in the comics, and is usually seen on the streets attempting to sell bootleg recordings of "[[The Avengers (2012 film)|The Incident]]".<ref>{{cite episode|title=Moment of Truth|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=McGuigan, Paul (director); Cheo Hodari Coker (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=September 30, 2016|season=1|number=1}}</ref> Cage approached him on the street and asked that he use his eyes to inform him about the goings on. Despite some reluctance, Dave gave in, acting as an informant.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Manifest|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Goddard, Andy (director); Akela Cooper (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=September 30, 2016|season=1|number=7}}</ref>  He appears in the episode "You Know My Steez", filming the street fight between Cage and [[Willis Stryker]].<ref>{{cite episode|title=You Know My Steez|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Johnson, Clark (director); Aida Mashaka Croal & Cheo Hodari Coker (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=September 30, 2016|season=1|number=13}}</ref> He returns in season 2 where he has set up a Luke Cage merchandise store within Pop's Barber Shop, much to Cage and Bobby Fish's chagrin. He follows Cage around by using the ''Harlem's Hero'' app and films his many exploits.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Soul Brother #1|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Liu, Lucy (director); Cheo Hodari Coker (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=1}}</ref> Following Cage's defeat at the hands of [[Bushmaster (John McIver)|Bushmaster]], Griffith begins selling tapes of the fight, despite still admitting support for Cage.<ref>{{cite episode|title=I Get Physical|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Richardson-Whitfield, Salli (director); Matthew Lopes (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=4}}</ref> Griffith is also present when [[Iron Fist (comics)|Danny Rand]] arrives to visit Cage and he even gets to see him briefly use his powers.<ref>{{cite episode|title=The Main Ingredient|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Goddard, Andy (director); Akela Cooper (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=10}}</ref> While at a party, Griffith and his girlfriend Aisha Axton witness two friends of theirs reacting violently to a drug labeled 'Bushmaster' and inform Cage.<ref>{{cite episode|title=Can't Front On Me|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Gout, Evarado (director); AÃ¯da Mashaka Croal (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=12}}</ref> When Cage makes a bid to act as the "sheriff" of Harlem, Griffith voices his concern. He ultimately decides to use the money he earned from his Luke Cage gift shop to buy Pop's Barber Shop stating that Harlem needs a Switzerland. He is last seen putting a "Help Wanted" sign up on the window.<ref>{{cite episode|title=They Reminisce Over You|series=Marvel's Luke Cage|credits=Lopez, Alex Garcia (director); Cheo Hodari Coker (writer)|network=[[Netflix]]|airdate=June 22, 2018|season=2|number=13}}</ref>
