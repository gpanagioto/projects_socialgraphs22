'''Yellowjacket''' is a fictional [[super-hero]], and the first to be published by the company that would become [[Charlton Comics]]. He first appeared in ''Yellowjacket Comics'' #1 (September 1944).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Markstein |first1=Don |website=Don Markstein's Toonopedia |access-date=2 April 2020  |url=http://www.toonopedia.com/yelojakt.htm |title=Yellowjacket}}</ref>

== Publication history ==
Yellowjacket starred in his own title, ''Yellowjacket Comics'', which was published for ten issues from 1944 to 1946.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Benton |first1=Mike |title=Superhero Comics of the Golden Age: The Illustrated History |date=1992 |publisher=Taylor Publishing Company |location=Dallas |isbn=0-87833-808-X |url=https://archive.org/details/superherocomicso0000bent |url-access=registration |access-date=1 April 2020 |page=[https://archive.org/details/superherocomicso0000bent/page/193 193]}}</ref> There was no artist or writer credited for the stories. These ten issues were published by "E. Levy/Frank Comunale", which later became Charlton. When the title was renamed ''Jack in the Box'', Yellowjacket appeared in the first issue, #11. He also appeared in ''TNT Comics'' #1, which was published by "Charles Publications".<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mougin |first1=Lou |title=Secondary Superheroes of Golden Age Comics |date=2020 |publisher=McFarland & Co |isbn=9781476638607|page=405}}</ref>

Due to copyright laws at the time of his inception, Yellowjacket lapsed into the public domain on the 28th anniversary of his initial published appearance.

Yellowjacket finally makes his [[DC Comics]] debut in [[Grant Morrison]]'s ''[[The Multiversity]]'' series. He is the first superhero on Earth 4, as well as the father of the [[President of the United States]] on that Earth.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/morrison-and-quitely-tackle-watchmen-and-modern-su-212146|title=Morrison and Quitely tackle Watchmen and modern superheroes in Pax Americana|date=21 November 2014|work=avclub.com}}</ref>

== Fictional character biography ==
Yellowjacket's secret identity is crime writer and amateur beekeeper Vince Harley.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Mitchell |first1=Kurt |last2=Thomas |first2=Roy |title=American Comic Book Chronicles: 1940-1944 |date=2019 |publisher=TwoMorrows Publishing |isbn=978-1605490892 |page=248}}</ref> After a group of jewel robbers attempted to kill him by pouring a box of [[yellowjacket]]s on him, he found that he had gained the ability to control the insects, and used that ability to fight crime while wearing a yellow costume with a black and yellow striped cape resembling the markings of a bee.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Yoe |first1=Craig |title=Super Weird Heroes:Outrageous But Real! |date=2016 |publisher=Yoe Books/IDW |isbn=978-1631407451 |page=300}}</ref> 

According to ''Jess Nevins' Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes'', "he fights ordinary criminals, insane surgeons, foreign agents, and mad architects".<ref>{{cite book |last1=Nevins |first1=Jess |title=Encyclopedia of Golden Age Superheroes |date=2013 |publisher=High Rock Press |isbn=978-1-61318-023-5 |page=300}}</ref>

In ''The Multiversity,'' he is shown to have been accidentally shot and killed by his son.
